📝 Abstract

Estimating internal biomechanical variables and muscle fatigue from visual observations remains a significant challenge in biomechanics, rehabilitation, and sports science. Conventional approaches rely on wearable sensors such as electromyography (EMG), inertial measurement units (IMUs), or motion-capture systems, which are often expensive, intrusive, and difficult to deploy outside controlled laboratory environments.

This work presents a vision-based musculoskeletal simulation framework for predicting biomechanical variables and muscle fatigue from ordinary RGB videos. The approach combines pose estimation, biomechanical modeling, and physics-based simulation in a unified pipeline. Human motion is captured with standard cameras and processed to extract body keypoints; kinematic features are reconstructed and integrated into an OpenSim musculoskeletal model to estimate joint torques, muscle activations, and muscle forces. A three-compartment fatigue model then characterizes muscle fatigue dynamics during exercise.

A case study on biceps curls generates a physics-informed dataset linking visual observations to internal biomechanical states. Experiments show that vision-derived kinematics accurately reproduce biomechanical quantities of interest while providing meaningful estimates of muscle activation and fatigue progression. The framework enables non-contact biomechanical monitoring and creates a foundation for future AI models that predict internal biomechanical variables directly from video — a low-cost, scalable solution for rehabilitation, ergonomics, sports performance, and remote healthcare.

🔬 Pipeline

1
Calibration — Fit3D cameras → Pose2Sim Calib.toml (translation = −R·T; took elbow r from 0.86 → 0.99).
2
RTMPose 2D — 26 HALPE keypoints / frame / view (4 cameras, 50 fps).
3
Triangulation — confidence-weighted multi-view → 3D joints (.trc); 27.8 mm vs Vicon.
4
Butterworth 2 Hz — zero-phase filtering → clean joint accelerations.
5
OpenSim Arm26 — scaled model → Inverse Dynamics, Static Optimization, 3-Compartment fatigue.
6
AI surrogate — LightGBM (+ Optuna + SHAP) replaces OpenSim in milliseconds.
